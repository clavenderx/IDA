Eva María Árnadóttir

Threading Icelandic Roots in Fashion

As Dean of the School of Architecture, Design and Fine Art at Iceland University of the Arts, she continues to influence the next generation of creatives while advocating for the growth and recognition of Icelandic fashion design.

By developing a deep connection to Icelandic textiles and the craft behind them. Through this experience, she discovered how clothing is not only something we wear but, it can express identity, reflect culture, and carry the stories of the places that inspire us.

She believes Icelandic fashion's originality comes from the challenges of being an island, far from manufacturing and production. These limitations have inspired people to reimagine constraints as opportunities for innovation.

"For me, fashion is not defined by a single style, but rather its an ongoing conversation between heritage and experimentation."
